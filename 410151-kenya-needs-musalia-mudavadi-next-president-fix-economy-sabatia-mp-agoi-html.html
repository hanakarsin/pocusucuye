Kenya Needs Musalia Mudavadi as Next President to Fix Economy, Sabatia MP Agoi

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Tuesday, May 28, 2024

- Sabatia MP Alfred Agoi expressed confidence Musalia Mudavadi is the missing link in Kenya's leadership

- The ANC leader identified the economy as priority areas he would focus on should he win the country's presidency in 2022

- In July 2020, ANC's National Governing Council unanimously settled on Mudavadi as its flag bearer for the 2022 contest

The Amani National Congress party (ANC) leader Musalia Mudavadi has singled out the economy as priority area he will focus on in his first presidential term.

The former vice president and the one-time Finance minister is upbeat that he would draw lessons he learnt when he was heading the Treasury to turn around the country's poor economy.

His political soldiers led by Sabatia MP Alfred Agoi and Nambale's Sakwa Bunyasi also expressed confidence the seasoned politician is the missing link in Kenya's leadership.

"It is clear that all eyes are now looking up to Musalia Mudavadi. Fixing the economy of this country being top of his agenda if given a chance by Kenyans, that is the leader Kenya needs," Agoi stated.

On his part, Bunyasi observed counties, unlike constituencies, are endowed with all the four production factors which can be harnessed for sustainable economic growth, which is a key pillar in the ANC manifesto.

"As ANC party, we are focused on building leadership that will give such quality for holistic growth in our counties," he said.

2022 endorsement

In July 2020, ANC's National Governing Council unanimously settled on Mudavadi as its flag bearer for the 2022 contest.

The former ANC secretary-general Barrack Muluka confirmed the party would only conduct party primaries for the other seats save for that of the presidential contender.

"One of the most critical resolutions of the meeting was the endorsement of Musalia Mudavadi as the party presidential flag bearer for the year 2022. It's now therefore official that the highest organ of the party had given Musalia to go out and begin soliciting for votes and support. There will be no other contenders for the party presidential seat," he said.

Following the development, Muluka said Mudavadi was now free to seek support among like-minded colleagues with a view to forging a formidable political alliance.

The ANC boss had hinted he was ready to work with any politicians including Deputy President William Ruto so long as he supported his presidential bid.

"We are tired of being told that we are working for William Ruto. Let me say that we welcome Dr Ruto as long as he supports our bid for the presidency,” he said.
