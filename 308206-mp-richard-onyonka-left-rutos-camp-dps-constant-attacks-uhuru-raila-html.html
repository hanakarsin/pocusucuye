MP Richard Onyonka says he left Rutos camp because of DP's constant attacks on Uhuru, Raila

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Saturday, June 15, 2024

- Onyanka was a close ally of Ruto and was the chairman of his Tanga Tanga faction in Kisii county

- The two leaders later parted ways and the MP has disclosed he was tired of DP's attacks on President Uhuru Kenyatta and Raila Odinga

- The vocal politician has since embraced the handshake and turned into a Ruto critic

A visibly vexed Kitutu Chache South MP Richard has claimed he parted ways with Deputy President William Ruto because of his endless attacks on President Uhuru Kenyatta and ODM leader Raila Odinga.

The vocal MP was one of the DP's close allies but has since turned into his fiercest critic, accusing him of being corrupt and disrespectful of other leaders.

Speaking to NTV on Monday, June 17, Onyonka said he got tired of Ruto's public rants directed the President and Opposition chief yet they were unifying factors in the country.

"I went to his house and told him three things; first, I asked him to stop talking about Raila Odinga, number two, I asked him to stop talking about Uhuru because his boys were doing it all over, lastly I told him if the president tells you to slow down, you slow down but he never listened that is why I left him publicly," he alleged.

Onyonka had previously indicated voters in his Kisii backyard were bitter with the DP and had advised him to abandon his Tanga Tanga faction.

Speaking in a funeral function in the region on Saturday, May 25, the lawmaker who was accompanied by Raila warned Ruto against abusing the former premier since "he was not his equal".

"Listen, I want to categorically tell Ruto that Raila is not your equal. Stop abusing Raila in your public functions," he said. "You cannot abuse Raila from morning to evening. Kenyans are tired with your political hatred. I want this message to reach you very clearly," Onyonka added.

The legislator further asked the deputy president to come out clean and explain to the public where money meant for the dams project went to and who loots public funds.

He accused Ruto of being a liability to Uhuru's government by derailing development agenda for his 2022 political interests.
