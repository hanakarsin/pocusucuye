Hair Regime: Why the need for different strength hair relaxers

Posted by Enda Jantzen on Tuesday, July 23, 2024

For a long time now, people have used women's hair to gauge their beauty standards and class.

There are so many ways that women have adopted to ensure that they look good but relaxing their hair is the most done.

Chemical hair relaxers are designed to straighten extremely curly, coiled or tightly coiled hair by breaking the di-sulfide bonds found within the cortex layer of the hair.

The level of hydroxide used in a relaxer determines the strength categorizing them into three; mild, super and regular.

Super strength contains a higher concentration of sodium hydroxide than a regular strength relaxer, although the result from a relaxer irrespective of strength, is straighter and more manageable hair.

The strength of the relaxer used to achieve the result is very important with the aim to avoid hair and scalp damage based on the hair texture.

It is advisable to observe the following rules;

Only use the highest strength (Super) for the most resistant healthy hair. Ideal for the 4c hair type

Use medium strength (Regular) for the average healthy hair. Ideal for the 4b hair type

Use the lowest strength (Mild) for the less resistant healthy hair. Ideal for the 4a hair type

During a chemical relaxing procedure of a hydroxide relaxer, a process called lanthionization occurs, which is the breaking of a disulfide bonds to alter the curl pattern of the hair.

During the process, the curl pattern is loosened or relaxed while the cortex layer of the hair is elongated, stretching the original curl pattern, making this a permanent alteration.

The introduction of relaxer kits, has made it more convenient, cost-effective and safer for women to straighten hair at home.

Despite the latest surge of natural hair trends, the true relaxer woman still stands her ground.

Relaxers remain the most effective way to achieve maximum permanent straightness while keeping the hair healthy, conditioned and manageable.
