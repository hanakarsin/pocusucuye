Kenyan newspapers review for October 30: CS Magoha changes tune again, to hold stakeholders meeting

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Friday, July 5, 2024

The contentious Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) continues to dominate headlines with President Uhuru Kenyatta and former prime minister Raila Odinga reported to be in a dilemma on how to fund a referendum.

The rising COVID-19 cases across the country have paralysed operations in several counties. Hundreds of county workers and ward representatives have been admitted in hospitals after a rise in the number of infections in what the Ministry of Health termed as a second wave.

1. Daily Nation

Deputy President William Ruto has softened his stance on BBI and said he is ready to dialogue with Uhuru and Raila after meeting with the National Council of Elders, headed by its patron Captain Kung’u Muigai on Tuesday at his Karen residence home.

The second in command who had insisted he needed more persuasion during the launch of the document on Monday, October 26, heeded to the elders proposal led by Uhuru's cousin Kung'u to build consensus for a non-contested plebiscite.

The DP was speaking in Narok county yesterday during the burial of Elijah Muterian Ole Kuyo (Oloboru Enkeene), the father to nominated senator Mary Seneta.

"During the launch of the BBI report, the president urged us to build consensus and reach out to each other," he said.

2. People Daily

Education Cabinet Secretary who on Wednesday, October 28, cautioned all school heads against reopening learning institutions for exclusive grades has changed his tune again.

The flip-flopping senior education official said plans were underway to develop a roadmap for resumption of classes for students in Grade One to Three, Class Five to Seven and Form One to Three.

He also insisted the ministry would not shut down schools despite the spike in COVID-19 cases across the country where some teachers and learners have been infected.

"We are going to meet with stakeholders soon and when the time comes, we may perhaps be bold enough, if we reach consensus, to tell you when the rest of the school will open," he said.

3. The Star

President Uhuru Kenyatta and Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) leader Raila Odinga were reported to be in a dilemma on how to fund a referendum.

While the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) maintained the process would cost KSh 14 billion, Raila dismissed the figure arguing the independent commission was out to "rip" Kenyan taxpayers.

"We should be able to conduct a one ballot referendum at no more than KSh 2 billion for 20 million voters," said Raila.

Acting IEBC chief executive officer Murjan Hussein Murjan told lawmakers on Wednesday, October 29, the KSh 14 billion amount may even shoot up depending on the number of new voters set to be registered ahead of the vote.

"We have on our own sat down and asked ourselves the budgetary requirements in the event of a possible referendum. We have already estimated that the referendum to change the Constitution will cost KSh 14 billion. However, we are still refining the budget,” he said.

4. The Standard

Amid the rising COVID-19 cases, it has emerged that university students are set to attend their online lessons in bars and hotels where they can access free internet according to vice chancellors.

The varsity heads met at during a conference organised by the Commission for University Education (CUE) and raised concerns over online learning and its challenges.

At the meeting, lack of laptops and internet connection was cited as the biggest problems faced by comrades thus limiting their class attendance.

"We must push for endorsement of online lessons by parents so children can attend classes from serene environments, " Zetech University VC Njenga Munene appealed.

5. Taifa Leo

The Ministry of Defence has used KSh 50 billion to combat terrorism along Kenya's border with Somalia and also in the coastal region.

Interior Principal Secretary Karanja Kibicho revealed the funds were used to purchase firearms that were used by Kenyan soldiers to deal with Al-Shabaab militants.

The powerful PS said their efforts had paid off as calm had return to Lamu and other parts of the Coast and North Eastern region.
