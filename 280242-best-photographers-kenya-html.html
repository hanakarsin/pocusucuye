Best photographers in Kenya - Tuko.co.ke

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Tuesday, February 20, 2024

They said a picture is worth a thousand words. How true that statement is the prerogative of the lenses that take these snaps. Since time immemorial, photos are the timelines, personal stories and priceless-filled memories we have treasured. These imageries preserve essential aspects of our lives. But then again, the guy behind the camera is the defining element that determines the value of the narrative in the photography. Kenya is proud to have a cache of professional photographers, that among other things, have been at the forefront in telling the Kenya own story to the world. From special events to nature and wildlife; who among them has earned a ticket deserving the title as among the best photographers in Kenya. Read on to learn.

Here are some of the best photographers in the country.

1. Ben Kiruthi

Ben’s journey in photography began after his wedding to his charming wife in 2014. From the desire to preserve the memories of her big day to a passionate career, Ben’s mission is to tell the nuptial narrative through the eyes of his lenses. He is one of the best photographers in Kenya. From wedding to destination photographer, he desires to tell love stories etched on the pieces of high-quality films that has propelled him to be among the leading photographer in his right.

His equally talented photographer, Martin Macharia (Matt Masha) captured the memorable moments at the Photographer’s wedding.

His 6 years stint at an Engineering programme at Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology couldn’t prepare him for what was in store in his later life. It was his enthusiast love for the camera that finally overcame his earlier career pursuits. With no skill in photography, he quit his job to pursue his calling. The first paycheck was a measly Kshs 7,000. Today he is famed to make over 0.5 million a month from his exploits.

Kiruthi describes himself as a Christian photographer whose significant accomplishment with his camera has earned him a global audience having been ranked a top high-end lensman in Kenya. He regards himself as a league of world’s most singled out wedding photographers. Together with his wife, they have successfully offered their services to over 100 weddings.

3. Antony Trivet

Antony has always sought to be a top-notch best wedding photographers in Kenya who attends to the varying needs of the client at the best price possible. He specializes in wedding photography that takes him to the far countries in East Africa.

He has successfully shot the most recognizable weddings styled with a traditional touch. Talk of projecting African culture into the modern global stage.

His creativity and attention to detail photography have won him several recognitions and accolades. He received nominations for countless awards. From the nomination, he successfully bagged the 2016 Canon Selphy Street Photography competition and 2015 Kenya Glamour Awards for the upcoming photographer. 4. Kevin Buo

Kevin goes by the name BuoArt. He kicked off his photography business through his flagship-BuoArt photography in 2011. Kevin usually concentrates on modeling, conceptual photography, lifestyle, and beauty. He is one of the best photographers in Nairobi Kenya being sought after by celebrities.

5. Mutua Matheka

Mutua’s fascination with art started in earnest when he was just three years. Using crayons, he sketched (literally) his career in photography through his drawings.

Matheka’s study in Architecture at Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture & Technology (J.K.U.A.T) carved his niche in photography. He is one of the top photographers in Kenya who are widely accredited for capturing the most fantastic photos of Kenya’s architecture, the popular landmark, cityscapes, and landscapes. His goal is to showcase the beautiful scenes of Nairobi and Africa’s skyline through the campaign-I’m a City Changer.

In 2012 the photographer was a nominee recipient of CSS design Award (US). He won the 2013 BAKE Best Photography Blog in Kenya award.

Also, he has featured in various documentaries and platforms like African Digital Art (ADA), Afri-love.com, BBC, and Zuqka magazine.

His riding craft in photography has brought on board large telecom and tech companies like Huawei and Safaricom. The Huawei-Safaricom initiative dubbed-‘Huawei voice’ sought to use Matheka’s skillful art to shoot snaps on top of Safaricom communication mast.

6. Siambi Brian (Urbanskript)

Brian Siambi began his journey in photography in 2014. What sets him apart from the rest is her immersion in capturing the African identity in black and white. His pursuits have led him to work with the likes of Adele Dejak and Chichia. Moreover, he also happens to be a graphic designer.

Brian’s ‘The Dark Matter ‘is the platform in which he nurtures his growing prowess in fine arts African fashion photography. His authentic style in his craft stems from his desire to showcase the glamour of the dark skin. This venture has propelled to be the top fashion photographers in Kenya and beyond the global fashion scene. Reputable brands like Vogue are haggling to have a pie to his talent.

His photography has paid a lot emphasis on African designers. Siambi’s work has made tremendous headways in popularizing the dark African complexion. This move is coming at an exciting time in Africa when the Melanin movement has seen a rise in African women's pride in their colored skin

7. Osborne Macharia

Macharia describes himself as a self-taught photographer who specializes in Afrofuturism art photography. The K63 studio is the flagship of his brilliant productions. Without a doubt, he is one of the best male photographers in Kenya.

His photos are thought-provoking, unique and unconventional. Osborne genius behind the lenses cuts across the genre that delves into African historical narrative fused in both culture, fantasy and fiction. This unique blend of art seeks to highlight the African identity.

His camera craft is famed for telling the African story to drive social change by bringing to light issues of gender inequality, environment, poaching, Albinism, the elderly to mention few. His retro-style form of photography has boosted his local and global standings.

Some of Osborne’s productions have been featured in media institutions like BBC, CNN, Huffing post and many more. Consequently, his famed camerawork has also steered him to work with various international brands like Coke, TV Base, Vodka, Samsung, Forbes, Pepsi, Kenya Airways and many more.

Macharia has been feted and recognized in various events. He made a mark in Kenya history by being the first Judge at Cannes Lions Awards.

As not enough, his photography has found its way on leading fashion magazines issues like the Marie Claire Netherlands (2017) and Vogue Italia. CSA Radar has also named as one of the 15 most influential photographers.

8. Emmanuel Jambo

Emmanuel is renowned for having a hand in fashion, wedding and commercial photography in Kenya and several other countries. He also finds time to immerse his talent in cinematography.

Emmanuel endeavors have catapulted him in the stage of fashion runways like the likes of London Fashion Week. His major specialty is a wedding and fine art photography.

In 2012 Forbes Africa magazine recognized him as a leading photographer in Africa

Jambo is most recognizable in Kenya for being the official Uhuru Kenyatta’s photographer. Leaders like the former presidents of Zambia-Kenneth Kaunda and Rupiah Banda have been his clients. Also, he has taken photos of his home country's president, South Sudan President Salva Kiir.

His sister's photography classes triggered Emanuel fascination with photography at a tender age of 9.

One of his most significant accomplishment was to shoot the wedding of the South Sudanese president’s daughter, Ugandan president Yoweri Museveni and a $6 million-worth royal Indian wedding.

He is currently the Managing Director of Emms Studios.

Allan is an independent filmmaker based in Nairobi. He has a liking for documentary, wedding, nature photography and photojournalism.

His consistent creative craft in photography saw him bag a shortlist for the award of the 2014 Sony World Photography best photo. The particular picture that won the hearts at the prize made him earn the position of top ten against 140,000 photographs spanning 166 countries.

As they say, the fruit does not fall away from the tree. Allan’s father who used to be a photographer for Getty Images (UK) shaped the budding Allan’s dreams, and since 2007 he has never looked back.

His craft was put to use at project 24 which tasked him to shoot imagery of the Nairobi life.

11. Daniel Irungu

Daniel has carved a particular niche in photography thanks to his unrivaled fondness for transforming special moments into am enduring memoirs. This Kenya–based photo man, loves to shoot landscape and travel snaps.
