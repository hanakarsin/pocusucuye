Terence Creative Treats Grandmother to Extreme Make-Over as Mother's Day Gift

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Sunday, February 18, 2024

- Terence Creative surprised his grandmother on Mother's Day by taking her to a salon for a make-over

- She was given hair and nails make-over before she was treated to a full course meal in a posh restaurant

- According to Terence, the idea to treat his grandmother was suggested by his wife Milly

Content creator Terence Creative treated his grandmother to a sweet extreme makeover during the Mother's Day celebration.

Terence and his wife Milly visited their grandmother at her residence and picked her up. afterwards, they drove her towards a posh salon in Westlands where she was treated to a mani-pedi and hair make-over.

The celebrity couple then shared the video on their YouTube channel showing fans how the journey started.

From the shared video, Terence also mentioned that he is building a house for their grandmother as she is still living in a rental place.

The saloonist who did the makeover was Terence's childhood neighbour who he grew up within Mathare.

Milly also chose her grandmother-in-law's make up style and asked the beautician to only use colourless nail polish on her nails as she did not want shouting colours on her nails.

She was given a foot and hand massage, before she was given the nail make-over, then she had her hair turned into an Afro style.

Afterwards, she had lovely make-up done on her face to compliment her darker complexion and highlight her facial features.

Then the three of them then proceeded to a sumptuous meal and the grandma can be seen enjoying the three-course meal.

While having a conversation over the meal, his grandmother shared that he used to drink but she quit in 1963.

Having made some money, the content creator said he chose to build a 3-bedroom house for her as a show of gratitude for the decades of sacrifice.

He says it was prudent that he starts with hers, then his will come later as the family was still young and can hold on slightly longer.

Terence also advised the youth to start their projects with the little money they have rather than waiting for a lump sum.
