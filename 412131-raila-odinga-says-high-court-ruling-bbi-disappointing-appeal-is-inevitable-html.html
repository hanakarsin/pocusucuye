Raila Odinga Says High Court Ruling on BBI Disappointing, Appeal Is Inevitable

- The ODM leader expressed his disappointment with the bench's decision but indicated the intention to appeal the judgment

- He also called on members of the public and politicians who the outcome of the court may have aggrieved to desist from attacking the judges

Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) leader Raila Odinga has broken his silence over the High Court's judgment that declared the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) process null and void.

An attempt by Raila and President Uhuru Kenyatta to amend the Constitution of Kenya through a popular initiative suffered a setback after a five-judge bench nullified the process.

In his statement on Saturday, May 15, the seasoned politician expressed his disappointment with the bench's decision but indicated the intention to appeal the judgment.

"Our constitution provides the right of appeal of a decision with which one feels aggrieved. That is precisely what we intend to do with the ruling of the five-judge bench on the BBI bill.....We will do so with sobriety and with respect for our judges and courts," he stated.

Raila also called on members of the public and politicians who the outcome of the court may have aggrieved to desist from attacking and disparaging the judges.

"In particular, I want to urge that we restrain from personalised attacks on the court and its members. We may disagree with the court but we must respect its ruling and its freedom to exercise its judgment as it understands the legal and constitutional matters before it," he added.

Judicial activism

On Friday, May 14, the BBI secretariat co-chairpersons David Waweru and Junet Mohamed termed the anti-BBI judgment as judicial activism, which they will challenge to end.

"Consequently, we are setting up a legal team to look into and appeal the ruling. In the meantime, we assure all Kenyans who believe in the BBI not to give up. It's not over yet, we still believe a new Kenya is possible. We will fight to the bitter end for that dream to be realised," the secretariat stated in a statement read by Junet.

The team questioned the wisdom behind stopping the president from initiating the bill, yet the president was not the promoter and the initiator of the BBI bill.

"The judges stopped the president who is not the promoter of the bill. As law-abiding leaders, we respect the decision of the court but we don't agree with it," the statement read.
