I want to form the government in 2022 and I welcome partners - Moses Wetang'ula

Posted by Enda Jantzen on Thursday, March 14, 2024

- Wetang'ula has declared his interest to vie for the top seat in the 2022 General Election

- He said electoral injustices needed to be addressed through law reforms for a peaceful Kenya

- The dethroned Senate Minority leader bitterly ended his partnership with NASA principal Raila

- The senator said he decided to go it alone because he was a victim of political betrayal

As the clock slowly ticks away to the 2022 General Election, talks of possible political alliances continue to hit headlines each single day and pundits hold that more will be witnessed.

On Thursday, August 2, troubled Bungoma Senator and FORD Kenya leader Moses Wetang'ula announced he was shopping for a potential partner with whom he intends to form the next government.

“In 2022, Wetang'ula wants to form government with others or others will form government with Wetang'ula,” he said when he visited St Mary’s High School in Busia.

The NASA co-principal said the electoral injustices in every election cycle must be addressed through reforming the law and the constitution and not through the Raila-Uhuru handshake

"The issue of two people shaking hands is fine, it reduces the temperature but it does not solve the problems that have brought us to where we were unless we address the serious issues,” he added.

Wetangula has since fallen out and ended his partnership with ODM leader Raila Odinga in what many see as a way to firm up his support base.

As reported previously by TUKO.co.ke, during a meeting with members of his party in Mombasa, the senator said he decided to go it alone because he was a victim of political brinkmanship and betrayal by Raila.

The dethroned former Senate Minority leader becomes the latest high profile politician to declare his presidential bid.

Others include Deputy President William Samoei Ruto and Baringo Senator Gideon Moi.
