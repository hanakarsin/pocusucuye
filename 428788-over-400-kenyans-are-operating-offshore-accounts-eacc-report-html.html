Over 400 Kenyans Are Operating Offshore Accounts, EACC Report

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Thursday, July 11, 2024
  • EACC divulged that at least 400 government officers opened offshore accounts over the last decade
  • This was after the 12th Parliament diluted the principle which barred state officials from maintaining a bank account outside Kenya
  • Parliament enacted the Leadership and Integrity Act with exemptions that allowed state officials to operate such accounts under conditions stipulated in the Act, including seeking clearance from EACC

The Ethics and Anti-Corruption Commission (EACC) has revealed that more than 400 Kenyans have offshore accounts.

According to EACC, at least 400 state officials opened such accounts over the last decade.

This was after the 12th parliament diluted the principle that barred public officers from maintaining a bank account outside Kenya.

Greenlight from EACCC

The parliament enacted the Leadership and Integrity Act with exemptions that allowed state officials to operate such accounts under conditions stipulated in the Act, including seeking clearance from EACC.

The Act says a state officer who has “reasonable grounds for opening or operating a bank account outside Kenya to apply to the Ethics and Integrity Commission for approval”.

According to EACC 2017-2018 annual report, at least 453 government officers were granted permission to own and operate secret accounts since 2012.

In a report file by the Daily Nation during the same period, the commission received applications and it processed eight notifications for bank account closures, four of which were executed in the 2017/18 financial year.

EACC added that it received and approved 15 applications from state officials to open or continue to operate bank accounts outside Kenya in the 2017-18 financial year.

Once a public officer secures the commission's green light to operate the overseas account, they are required to submit annual bank statements to the commission not later than January 31 of each succeeding year.

However, not all government officers who have oversees accounts complied with these rules,

EACC divulged that it was also investigating 191 public officers operating secret bank accounts abroad illegally.

The commission added that 58 officers had failed to furnish the commission with their annual bank statements.

Mutua says he once had offshore account

In a related story, TUKO.co.ke reported Governor Alfred Mutua admitted owning offshore accounts before joining active politics and vying for the Machakos gubernatorial seat in 2012.

Speaking to Citizen TV on Thursday, October 7, Mutua said he was compelled to close all his offshore accounts as per the law.

''The Constitution says in Section 76 (2) (a) that a state officer shall not maintain a bank account outside Kenya except per an Act of Parliament. So when I was running for the Machakos gubernatorial seat, I closed all my bank accounts overseas,'' Mutua said.
