New Education CS George Magoha warns late comers during impromptu visit to Jogoo House

Posted by Hettie Henneman on Tuesday, July 16, 2024

The no nonsense Education Cabinet Secretary George Magoha has already hit the ground running with a stern warning to lazy employees at the ministry's headquarters who have a tendency of coming to work late.

The CS was left in shock after he visited one of the offices at Jogoo House only to find empty chairs yet the few workers who were available could not explain where their colleagues were way past the reporting time.

In a video shared on Facebook, the tough talking CS who spoke to those he found in the office said if their colleagues were tired with work, he was ready to get other hardworking Kenyans to replace them.

"If I start coming here at 10am, what do you think the rest will do? Obviously they will also start reporting at 10am, people have to sit and work, otherwise I will remove them, they can go to another ministry where they can sleep. Education Ministry consumes all government money and our working will directly impact on the president's quality of work," he said. ""You (the ones who were in office) keep working that way, but the rest... I thought I could warn one or two but now they are becoming too many, over 100 of them, you can't warn 100 people," added Magoha.

Some of the employees hid in corners when they heard the CS was in the office.

"If someone is sick or they call in and say they are coming late for some reason, then I can understand. But these people are so used to coming in late. This habit has to stop today, it is not my duty to supervise you everyday," he added.

The 66-year-old was appointed to replace Amina Mohammed who was moved to the sports docket following the sacking of Rashid Echesa.

Among other key changes the father of one is expected to implement fully include the new education curriculum and proper management of national exams.
