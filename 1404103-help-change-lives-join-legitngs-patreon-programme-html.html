Help us change lives, join Legit.ngs Patreon programme

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Monday, January 1, 0001

A couple of years ago, Legit.ng started telling stories of people in Nigeria and through our readers’ support, we have impacted a number of lives together.

But we believe the time has come for more lives to be changed and in our quest to ensure this happens, Legit.ng joined Patreon, a crowdfunding platform that enables fans (patrons) to pay and support content creators.

PAY ATTENTION: Join Legit.ng's Patreon programme by clicking this link

Legit.ng’s Youtube channel has steadily grown its subscriptions to over 187,000 subscribers since it was established in 2017 due to its quality coverage of human interest and charity stories while also covering other fields.

We pride ourselves in ensuring that people’s needs are heard. But like many other content creators, the major challenge remains inability to achieve sustainable resources to continue to tell these stories and more.

During a recent trip to Calabar, the Cross River state capital in one of our journeys to tell people stories, Legit.ng discovered the living conditions of the vulnerable children on the streets.

We captured their living conditions, mental state and their means of survival. We learnt that these children wear clothes they find in the dustbin and live by means of collecting metal in the streets.

With your support, Legit.ng plans to change the lives of these children and many more kids and families out there.

This is why we urge you to join our Patreon programme as Legit.ng seeks to fund more life changing programmes with our fans’ support.

Why become a Legit.ng patron?

Legit.ng embraced Patreon to build and transform communities while giving our fans the opportunity to be part of it.

With your donations starting from as little as 5 dollars, we can continue to impact lives and change the society together, starting with the vulnerable children on the streets of Calabar.

We promise complete transparency and accountability for the trust you place in us, and every month inside our Patreon, we will be publishing detailed reports so that every benefactor can see how much of an impact they made.

As Legit.ng’s parton, you will get exclusive benefits like:

1. Early Access to Legit.ng content

Legit.ng has proven time and again that timely coverage of events, verifying and disseminating information with a human touch is a service it owes its audiences.

This is why those coming on board as patrons will be able to access our exclusives and high quality contents in due time before any other person.

2. Access to customised gifts

Your commitment and generosity to associate with Legit.ng and help us touch more lives and tell more stories that have been untouched due to financial limitations will be rewarded.

Patrons will get access to lovely gifts from us like branded Legit.ng hoodies, t-shirts, notebooks and other amazing stuff.

3. Access to live sessions with Legit.ng presenters

Legit.ng patrons will have the opportunity to interact with their favourite presenters on live sessions and get to reach millions of audiences through our established platforms online.

4. Special recognition for our patrons on their special occasions

Should you be of the Legit.ng family, patrons will get a special recognition on their birthdays, anniversaries and other special events from team members.

Here are four Legit.ng patrons' levels and their benefits:

1. Helping hand

Patrons subscribe through paying a monthly donation of $5.

100% of the proceedings will be used to procure basic necessities for the homeless children of Calabar.

In this category, patrons will enjoy behind-the-scenes contents, early access to our video contents, and be a supporter in our hall of fame.

2. Friend of Calabar

Patrons subscribe through paying a monthly donation of $10.

This category is for those who wish to support our work and help Legit TV crew afford trips and our work activities.

Your donations will help fund our activities, along with procuring supplies for the homeless children of Calabar.

3. Chief

Head of Video Desk at Legit.ng, Damilare Okunola, observed that there are many human interest stories that remain untold at the moment due to limited revenue flow.

“Travelling across the country, crossing the Niger, and going through cities would have been impossible without adequate and proper funding.

“And the monies that our videos generate, via social media platforms, are not even enough for two trips (we embark on a minimum of ten planned trips annually).

“This is the more reason we need more support from our followers who have watched us grow over the years.

"Your words of motivation and commendation have urged us on. We do hope for your financial support at this time, to do even more,” Damilare Okunola said.
