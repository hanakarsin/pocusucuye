15 well-made slavery movies that you need to watch in 2021

Posted by Almeda Bohannan on Saturday, February 24, 2024

Slavery has been existing in different societies across the world since time immemorial. Historians and enslaved folk have been documenting the atrocities through various media like books, films, and documentaries. Slavery movies are not always exciting to watch, especially those that are based on a true story.

Actual slavery movies elicit intense emotions and feelings. Many of the films are adaptations from books or accounts from slaves that chronicled their journeys. The primary purpose of such films is to educate the masses on the carnage caused by slave owners and make people aware of past atrocities so that history does not repeat itself.

15 well-made slavery movies

Slavery is perhaps one of the biggest blots of humanity’s existence. The macabre evil evokes the most loathsome responses from people, even when it is mentioned in hindsight. Here are the top 15 well-made slavery movies:

Gladiator is slightly based on actual events within the Roman Empire in the latter half of the 2nd century AD. In ancient Rome, gladiators were slaves. They were subjected to thorough training, fed on a high-energy diet, and were required to be energetic all the time.

Gladiator is among the best slavery movies on Netflix. The film features Russell Crowe, Joaquin Phoenix, Connie Nielsen, and Oliver Reed, among other characters.

2. Django Unchained (2012)

The 2012 Quentin Tarantino movie is not based on a true story, but it took elements from history to tell the story of Django, Schultz, and Candie. The movie is set in the American South just two years before the Civil War.

It follows Django, a slave freed and trained as a bounty hunter by German-born Dr King Schultz, who is disgusted by the racist ways of the South. The primary cast of Django Unchained was Jamie Foxx, Leonardo DiCaprio, Christoph Waltz, Samuel L. Jackson, and Kerry Washington.

This is one of the true slavery movies set in the American South during the civil war and reconstruction. Slavery was rampant, and it is among the major themes in the film even as viewers follow the romance and drama of a manipulative woman.

Harriet is based on excellent freedom fighter Harriet Tubman. The film follows Tubman as she escaped slavery and freed other slaves alongside her. The Birth of a Nation is one of the new slavery movies that you should watch. The film is based on the story of Nat Turner, a slave who was also a preacher, as he led a slave rebellion in Southampton County, Virginia, in 1831.

The story tells of life for African Americans in the 1800s and the racial stereotypes on them. The Birth of a Nation stars Nate Parker, Armie Hammer, Gabrielle Union, and Aja Naomi King, among other main characters.

Hollywood movies about slavery-like Uncle Tom's Cabin, will make you despise anyone that has ties to slave owners. The film is adapted from the Harriet Beecher Stowe novel.
