NCIC considering charging Junet Mohamed for claiming Isaac Mwaura bleached skin

Posted by Lourie Helzer on Tuesday, July 30, 2024

- NCIC said claims by Junet that Mwaura had bleached his skin were demeaning towards people with different skin colour

- The remarks, NCIC said, were likely to breed hatred between people of different skin colour if left unattended

- Junet made the remarks in disapproval of Mwaura's decision to join the wheelbarrow brigade associated with DP Ruto

The National Cohesion and Integration Commission (NCIC) has said it is considering taking action against Suna East MP Junet Mohamed after he made "demeaning" remarks against Jubile Party nominated senator Isaac Mwaura.

In a statement, NCIC termed the sentiments made by Junet as comments that could snowball into division between people of different colours "if left unattended".

The commission was reacting to Junet's claims that Mwaura, who serves as National Coordinator for Albinism Society of Kenya since 2007, "bleached" his skin for him to get nominated in parliament.

"The National Commission and Integration Commission condemns these utterances by Hon Junet Mohamed making fun of Hon Mwaura Isaac's disability. Such utterances are demeaning to persons living with disability, if left unattended it may trigger division between persons of different colours. The Commission is considering the matter with the view of taking appropriate action," NCIC said in a statement.

In the statement, NCIC tagged the Office of Director of Public Prosecution (ODPP), Interior CS Fed Matiang'i, State House Kenya and Government Spokesperson.

As separately reported by TUKO.co.ke, sentiments by Junet were heavily criticised by netizens and among senior government official to lashed out at him was Makueni Governor Kivutha Kibwana.

"The ODM party and Raila Odinga have consistently supported persons with disability. He was very vocal on this in the 90s at Bomas. This I must say of him. Therefore his supporters must be extremely careful about what they say about PWDs," said Kibwana.

In his comments, Junet expressed disapproval of a move by Mwaura to join Deputy President William Ruto's Wheelbarrow brigade and wondered "which coloured he will bleach into next" that he can benefit from Ruto's goodies.

"It seems he bleached himself so he could secure himself nomination to the National Assembly first through the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM), then Jubilee and now he has decamped to the wheelbarrow movement...we want the constitution amended so that all white men can be compared to people with disability," Junet sensationally said.

In a separate story, TUKO.co.ke reported that Mwaura was summoned to Jubilee Party's disciplinary committee where he faced among other charges disloyalty to the ruling party.

Mwaura, who arrived at the Pangani based Jubilee Pary headquarters with dozens of his supporters, dismissed charges levelled against him.

He went ahead and stated that he "had no faith in the committee" claiming that the party organ "already had settled on predetermined decision" of expelling him.
