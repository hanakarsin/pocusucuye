Samia Suluhu Appoints Opposition Presidential Candidate Queen Sendiga as Iringa Provincial Commissio

- Sindiga was a member of the ruling party, Chama Cha Mapinduzi (CCM), until 2014 when she ditched the outfit for the Alliance for Democratic Change

- Also appointed a provincial commissioner was the retired CCM politician Makongoro Nyerere

One of the presidential candidates in the 2020 Tanzanian General Election has landed a government job.

Queen Cuthbert Sendiga, who ran on the Alliance for Democratic Change (ADC) ticket, was appointed provincial commissioner by President Samia Suluhu Hassan on Saturday, May 15.

The Opposition politician will now be in charge of Iringa province, taking over from Ally Hapi, who moved to Tabora province in the same capacity.

Sindiga was a member of the ruling party, Chama Cha Mapinduzi (CCM), until 2014, when she ditched the outfit for ADC.

The following year, she unsuccessfully ran for a parliamentary seat.

Also appointed a provincial commissioner included the retired CCM politician and former president Julius Nyerere's son Makongoro Nyerere (Manyara province).

Ready to join hands

The president had in April assured the country that she was ready to protect democracy and fundamental freedoms.

Speaking during a maiden televised policy speech before parliament on Thursday, April 22, the president also said she would soon hold talks with opposition leaders.

Suluhu, who became Tanzania's first female president in March 2021, said she would discuss with the opposition how best they will conduct their political activities for the benefit of the country.

“Democracy, individual freedom and freedom of the press are important to foster development and peace. In the interest of protecting democracy, I intend to meet with all political party leaders to discuss how they can best conduct their political activities for the benefit of our country," the president said.
