Uhuru Kenyatta directs remaining group of learners to resume studies in January 2021

Posted by Chauncey Koziol on Wednesday, March 27, 2024

- Uhuru said those already in school will continue with their studies uninterrupted even as the rest wait for January next year

- Grade Four, Class Eight and Form learners re-joined school in October 2020 despite COVID-19 fears

- Several schools have been closed after they reported positive coronavirus cases with some teachers dying as a result

- CS Magoha has faced backlash and pressure to close down schools following the surge

President Uhuru Kenyatta has directed the group of learners that is yet to resume studies to prepare for reopening in early January 2021.

Grade Four, Class Eight and Form Four students reported back to school in October 2020 in strict adherence to COVID-19 health protocols.

Despite the surging numbers of infections, including in schools, Uhuru said learners who are already in class will proceed with learning.

He asked the Ministry of Education and school administrators to start making preparation to accommodate the second cadre of learners before January 2021.

Education CS George Magoha has been under immense pressure to close schools following a spike in infections involving teachers, students and other staffers.

At least three schools have been closed after recording cases of coronavirus.

TUKO.co.ke reported on an incident at Star of the Sea and Tononoka secondary schools where teachers and learners tested positive for the deadly disease, resulting in the closure of the two learning institutions.

Less than a week after the closure, the school Principal of Tononoka Secondary School died of COVID-19.

On Wednesday, November 4, Voi Primary School was closed after three teachers were diagnosed with coronavirus.

Other schools that have so far recorded cases of the viral disease include Friends School Kamusinga, Maranda High.

Despite this surge, the government has maintained learning will continue as planned.
