Ugandan Men Praying for Safe Landing of Female Afghanistan Refugees Catch Minister's Attention

Posted by Enda Jantzen on Sunday, March 3, 2024
  • Uganda is expected to play host to at least 2000 refugees escaping from the political crisis in Afghanistan
  • The East African nation is host to more than 1.45 million refugees, mostly from South Sudan, the Democratic Republic of Congo, Rwanda and Burundi
  • Afghanistan has been rocked by political unrest following the takeover by Taliban militants on Sunday, August 15

Ugandan men have taken to social media to hilariously welcome Afghanistan women following reports that the country will temporarily host 2,000 Afghan refugees.

The excited men took to Twitter to pray for the safe landing of female Afghanistan refugees, a move that has caught Minister of Gender, Labour and Social Development Betty Amongi's attention.

Supporting the gov't

The Ugandan men have promised total support and cooperation with the government to accommodate the refugees.

On Wednesday, August 18, Amongi said that she hopes the men will stick to their promises.

"I have been seeing our men here in Uganda pray for safe landing of Afghanistan refugees at Entebbe International Airport and even sharing various photos of ladies. They are promising total support and cooperation with government this time on this project! Hope it's not Stingy Men Association," Amongi posted on Twitter.

Afghanistan has been rocked by political unrest following the takeover by Taliban militants on Sunday, August 15.

On Tuesday, August 17, Uganda's State Minister for Relief, Disaster Preparedness and Refugees Esther Anyakun said that President Yoweri Museveni had agreed to grant refugee status to people fleeing Afghanistan following a request by the US government.

Anyakun said preparations to host the refugees started on Monday, August 16.

"We expect to host them temporarily before they can be relocated elsewhere by the US government. It was request from US government to His Excellency. We started the preparation on Monday, August 16," Anyakun told journalists.

The refugees had been expected on Monday, August 16

She added that the first batch of about 500 Afghan refugees was expected on Monday, August 16, but they did not arrive.

"We are expecting they might come any time since itâ€™s an emergency landing. They have requested us to host 2,000. They will be received in shifts," she explained.

According to the government, the US administration will meet all expenses incurred during their (refugees) stay.

Uganda is host to more than 1.45 million refugees, mostly from South Sudan, the Democratic Republic of Congo, Rwanda and Burundi.

According to the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR), Uganda has hosted refugees from outside Africa, 7,000 Polish refugees who arrived in the East African nation after the second world war.
