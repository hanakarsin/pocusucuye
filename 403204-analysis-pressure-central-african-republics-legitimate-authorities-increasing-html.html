Analysis: Pressure on Central African Republic's legitimate authorities is increasing

Posted by Enda Jantzen on Monday, June 17, 2024

More and more foreign mercenaries are infiltrating into the Central African Republic (CAR) to build up tension, escalate violence, do subversive activities and unleash hostilities on the ground.

The main goal of the rebels' accomplices is to capture the country's capital, Bangui.

Last month irregular troops attacked Bangui, killing at least two UN peacekeepers. It was the most serious threat to the capital since 2013 when the predominantly Muslim Seleka rebels seized power.

The factions are sure that this city's seizure will force the legally elected President Faustin-Archange Touader to resign and the army to surrender. However, another attack on the capital of CAR was successfully repulsed.

Last week marked two years since the CAR’s government signed a peace deal with rebel groups in an attempt to end years of fighting, but the country faces growing violence that threatens to make the agreement null and void.

“Instead of celebrating the dividends of peace, we are celebrating the second anniversary in a context of war,” said Presiden Touadera

Despite the violence, he said the agreement was not void and urged parties to stay faithful to it while assuring civilians that the army is doing what it can to reclaim rebel-held areas.

"Political-military groups had crossed the 'red line', have abandoned the agreements signed in Khartoum and can no longer be part of the political process. The leaders of these factions should now be outlawed. Central African Armed Forces (FACA) successfully attacks them in several directions, and they retreat from their positions. We have already managed to take control of the road connecting the capital with Cameroon. In the near future, the government will control the entire territory of CAR and peace will return to the regions. I appeal to the members of the groups - the only way out for you is to handcuff your leaders and hand them over to law enforcement agencies. Peace will come to the Central African Republic and development will begin. The citizens of this beautiful country deserve all this," Valery Zakharov, the security adviser to President Touadera said in a communiqué.

On Friday, thousands of people participated in a demonstration in Bangui in gratitude for Russian instructors assisting the governmental army in liberating the highway connecting the country with Cameroon and the cities from militants.

The rebels had cut off access between the capital and the Cameroonian border, where more than 1,600 trucks had been blocked since mid-December, causing food prices to spike.

Indeed, the Russian military's involvement has played a key role in pushing the foreign fighters and terrorists back.

Military experts also note that Russian instructors and military specialists, who have been long cooperating with CAR, have trained the local army well enough to cope with terrorist attacks and maintain order.

Against this background, international experts note the weak position of international organisations, their inaction, and their ineffectiveness.

Thus, on February 9, during a visit to Kigali (Rwanda), CAR's Minister of Foreign Affairs Sylvie Baïpo-Temon accused the international organisations of lacking political will and courage.

She condemned the start of negotiations between the international organisations and the armed groups.

Temon noted that the UN replaced the League of Nations when it had failed to prevent World War II, and wondered if it was time to replace the UN, which is fighting to maintain peace, as more conflicts have arisen since its inception.

According to the U.N, the mineral-rich nation faces an increasingly dire humanitarian crisis, with some 200,000 people fleeing their homes in less than two months.
